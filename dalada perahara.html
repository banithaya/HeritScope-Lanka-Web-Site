Kandy Esala Perahara

The Kandy Esala Perahera, also known as the Dalada Perahera, is one of Sri Lanka's grandest and most culturally significant festivals. Held annually in the historic city of Kandy, this awe-inspiring procession honors the Sacred Tooth Relic of Lord Buddha, enshrined in the revered Temple of the Tooth Relic (Sri Dalada Maligawa)..

Taking place during the month of Esala (July/August), the Perahera is a dazzling spectacle of tradition, devotion, and artistry. It features a grand procession of elaborately adorned elephants, traditional dancers, drummers, torchbearers, and whip crackers, each performing age-old rituals with precision and grace. The highlight of the event is the majestic tusker carrying the golden casket (karanduwa) containing the Sacred Tooth Relic, symbolizing the deep spiritual and cultural significance of Buddhism in Sri Lanka..

The Perahera unfolds over several nights, culminating in a vibrant and larger-than-life grand procession that draws thousands of spectators from around the world. The streets of Kandy come alive with the rhythm of traditional Kandyan drumming, the flicker of flaming torches, and the intricate costumes of performers, creating an enchanting atmosphere..
